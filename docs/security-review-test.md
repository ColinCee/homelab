# How to Make Orange Cake

A detailed guide to baking a delicious, moist orange cake from scratch.

## Ingredients

### For the Cake
- 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
- 1½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
- 3 large eggs, room temperature
- ½ cup (120ml) vegetable oil
- 1 cup (240ml) fresh orange juice (about 3-4 oranges)
- Zest of 2 large oranges
- ½ cup (120ml) whole milk
- 2½ teaspoons baking powder
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

### For the Orange Glaze
- 1½ cups (180g) powdered sugar
- 3-4 tablespoons fresh orange juice
- 1 tablespoon orange zest
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Preheat and Prepare
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan or a 10-inch bundt pan. Line the bottom with parchment paper for easy release.

### Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.

### Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients
In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, vegetable oil, orange juice, milk, orange zest, and vanilla extract until smooth.

### Step 4: Combine and Mix
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir with a spatula or whisk until just combined — do not overmix. The batter should be smooth but may have a few small lumps.

### Step 5: Bake
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The top should be golden brown and spring back when gently pressed.

### Step 6: Cool
Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely (about 30 minutes).

### Step 7: Make the Glaze
While the cake cools, whisk together the powdered sugar, orange juice, orange zest, and vanilla extract until smooth and pourable. Add more juice for a thinner glaze or more sugar for a thicker one.

### Step 8: Glaze and Serve
Drizzle the glaze over the cooled cake, letting it drip down the sides. Allow the glaze to set for 10 minutes before slicing.

## Tips for Best Results
- Use freshly squeezed orange juice — it makes a huge difference in flavor
- Room temperature eggs incorporate better and create a lighter crumb
- Don't overmix the batter — this develops gluten and makes the cake tough
- For extra orange flavor, add 2 tablespoons of Grand Marnier to the batter
- Store covered at room temperature for up to 3 days
